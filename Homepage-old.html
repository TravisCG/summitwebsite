Welcome to the SummitDB The SummitDB is a collection of processed ChIP-seq data. It is intended to provide information about possible direct or indirect interaction between transcription regulatory proteins and their positioning on the genome

TFBS view: View a genomic region you are interested in. You can check which motifs are involved and what kind of proteins are occupied on this region. Zoom on a motif to examine the identified ChIP-seq summits and their relative distance from the motif centre. The results can be filtered based on cell or protein type. Motif view: Explore the occupied proteins and their position (related to reference points: the centre of motif of interest) on a given motif. If you want to get detailed information about a specific transcription factor motif in global manner, the scatter plots can summarize for you the following information:
-Which ChIP-seq experiments overlap with the given type of motif (globally) and itâ€™s neighbouring 50 base pairs?
-What is the frequency of overlaps?
-What are the median/average summit positions of each overlapping ChIP-seq experiments around the motif?
-What is the standard deviation of summit positions?
Jbrowser: Browse genomic data including: motif locations, summit positions, ref genes etc. on hg19 reference genome. The genome browser is a graphical interface for rapid visualization of ChIP-seq data. Pair shift view: Check the linkage between two factors with comparing their summit distance distribution around a given motif. The comparison of two ChIP-seq experiments (which derive from the same cell line) can clarify the incidence of co-binding and the possible positioning of proteins. You can compare directly the summit distance distributions of two ChIP-seq experiment in the immediate vicinity of a motif type of interest. This view provides detailed information about the co-binding frequency between two target proteins in a given cell type.



Combine the results of different views. Check in motif view which ChIP-seq experiment shows the biggest overlap with a given motif than compare the its distance distribution with the ChIP-seq experiement of the transcription factor belongs to the motif (and derive from the same cell line) in pair shift view. Examine the individual motif instances where co-binding could be found in Jbrowser. Zoom on a single motif and investigate in TFBS region view what further factors are colocalized in the defined cell line.
